CATALOGUE OF COMMUNICATION 05.2018

Public space is filled with messages of all kinds written and performed by the individuals that inhabit it. As a result of these instances of self-expression, different forms of written and oral language have been born that have gone unaccounted for in the history of communication. This catalogue is an attempt to record and organize them and give them the attention they deserve.

THE PRICE OF DYING 12.2018

What would you like to have happen to your body when you die? In the Netherlands, there are currently only two legal options: cremation or burial. According to Dutch law, you must be buried within five days of your death. Most of us have the luxury of not having to think about our death too often, but it is good to keep in mind the fact that dying can be very expensive, for yourself, for your family, for your friends or even for the country in which you live. No matter what, if you die, someone will be paying money to dispose of your body. This publication explores the particular¬ities of the Dutch funerary environment and its current costs and laws.

SHH BE QUIET. WE ARE ENTERING A HOLY PLACE 12.2018

In an age of skyscrapers and high-rise buildings, it is nearly impossible to view the enormity of Cathedrals with the mindset of a medieval pilgrim. Yet, even from a 21st-century perspective, it is hard not to be moved by the sheer size and scale of the massive stone structures. This publication recounts the experience of 10 different tourists as they enter cathedrals while on holiday. All of the typefaces used have been distilled from carvings in different cathedrals that I’ve visited in Spain and the south of France.
